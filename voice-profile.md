# Potty Pal Voice Profile
> Parent-to-parent editorial voice. Warm, practical, honest. Never preachy. Never clinical.

---

## 1. Core Identity

**Who's talking:** A parent who's been through potty training with their own kids, did way too much research on it, and now helps other parents skip the panic. Not a pediatrician. Not a mommy blogger. A real parent who knows the mess is temporary and the wins are worth celebrating.

**Relationship to reader:** Trusted friend. The parent at the playground who gives you the advice you actually need, not the judgment you're afraid of.

**Brand voice in three words:** Warm. Honest. Practical.

## 2. Tone Calibration

| Dimension | Setting |
|-----------|---------|
| Formal <-> Casual | 75% casual. Conversational. Contractions always. |
| Expert <-> Beginner | Knows the research but explains it like a friend over coffee. |
| Serious <-> Playful | 50/50. Lighthearted about the chaos, serious about the child's wellbeing. |
| Reassuring <-> Urgent | 80% reassuring. Parents are already stressed. Calm them down. |
| Salesy <-> Editorial | 90% editorial. CTA is a gentle mention, never a pitch. |

## 3. The Voice in Action

### YES
> "Your kid had five dry days in a row and then peed on the couch. That's not failure. That's potty training."

> "Regression is your toddler's brain saying 'I have too many new things happening right now.' It passes."

> "You don't need a $200 potty training course. You need a plan, some patience, and probably more underwear than you think."

### NO (Kill on sight)
> "Potty training can be a challenging journey for many families." (Generic filler)
> "We're passionate about helping parents navigate this milestone." (Brand speak)
> "Unlock your child's potty training potential." (AI garbage)
> "As parents, we understand the struggles you face." (Condescending)

## 4. Sentence Architecture

- **Short (2-8 words):** ~30%. For landing points and reassurance. "It's normal." / "This works." / "Give it a week."
- **Medium (10-20 words):** ~55%. One idea per sentence. The workhorse.
- **Long (20-30 words):** ~15%. For storytelling and scenario-setting only.
- **Rhythm:** Build, build, LAND. Two setup sentences, then a short one that drives it home.
- **Fragments welcome:** "Not the potty. The timing." / "Totally normal." / "Three days. That's it."

## 5. Paragraph Rules

- Max 4 sentences per paragraph. Most should be 2-3.
- One-sentence paragraphs for emphasis are encouraged.
- **Open with a scenario**, not a definition: "It's 7 AM. Your toddler is crying because they want their diaper back."
- **Never open with:** "In this article..." / "Welcome to..." / "Potty training is defined as..."

## 7. Content Rules

- **Be specific:** "Try bare-bottom time for 2-3 hours each morning" beats "give them time without a diaper."
- **Name real scenarios:** Don't say "during transitions." Say "when a new baby arrives" or "the first week of daycare."
- **Include ages:** "Around 22 to 30 months" is more useful than "when they're ready."
- **Normalize the mess:** Accidents, regression, and frustration are part of the process. Never shame.
- **Honest about limits:** If something doesn't work for every kid, say so. Parents trust us because we're honest.
- **Medical disclaimer:** Footer says "Educational coaching, not medical advice." Don't play doctor in the content either. Flag when to call a pediatrician.

## 8. CTA Rules

- No hard sells. The app is mentioned as a helpful tool, not a miracle product.
- Vary phrasing every post. Never repeat a CTA across posts.
- Frame helpfully: "That's what we built Potty Pal for" not "Download now!"
- Skip it if it doesn't fit.

## 9. Voice Checklist (Pre-Publish)

- [ ] Opens with a scenario or relatable moment, never a definition
- [ ] Zero banned words or phrases
- [ ] No paragraphs over 4 sentences
- [ ] Contractions used throughout
- [ ] At least one specific, actionable tip with a number or timeframe
- [ ] At least one reassuring moment ("This is normal" energy)
- [ ] Reads like advice from a friend, not a textbook or a sales page
